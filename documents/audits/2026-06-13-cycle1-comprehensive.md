## Executive summary
The codebase is fundamentally healthy: it has a well-defined logging convention, an explicit concurrency discipline, and a documented immutability standard for `@unchecked Sendable` — and it largely follows them. The dominant risk pattern is **observability debt**: raw `print()` leaking into production error paths across both macOS and iOS targets, plus a cluster of `Task.detached` calls with implicit `self` capture that drift from the project's own explicit-capture rule. The single most consequential correctness item is the `MCPServersViewModel` capture-list issue (a Swift 6 strict-mode violation). **No** security/injection findings, **no** writes to `state.db` (read-only invariant holds), **no** cross-feature imports, and **no** `ScarfCore` AppKit/UIKit platform leaks were found.

- **Counts (authoritative, programmatic):** critical **0** / high **4** / medium **15** / low **10** — 29 confirmed, **15 refuted** during verification.
- **Caveat — heavy cross-dimension duplication:** the raw `print()` family was independently reported by 7 of the 8 dimensions. Deduplicated, the 29 findings collapse to **~10 distinct actionable issues** (see the queued tasks).

## Critical & high-severity findings (prioritized)

- **`Task.detached` missing explicit `self` capture in `MCPServersViewModel`** — `scarf/scarf/Features/MCPServers/ViewModels/MCPServersViewModel.swift` (lines 96, 118, 136, 148, 165, 207, 240, 265, 279) — nine detached tasks reference `self`/its properties inside `MainActor.run` blocks without a capture list; under Swift 6 strict concurrency this crosses the detached→MainActor isolation boundary without an explicit capture, and line 63 already establishes the correct `[weak self]` pattern. *(dimension: concurrency)* **Fix:** add `[weak self]` (or `[self]` where lifetime is guaranteed) to all nine calls. *Note: the app builds today, so under the project's current `SWIFT_APPROACHABLE_CONCURRENCY` setting this surfaces as a warning rather than a hard error — but it is the project's own rule.*
- **Raw `print()` in iOS WebView delegate (navigation failure)** — `scarf/Scarf iOS/Projects/Widgets/WebviewWidgetView.swift:117` — shipped iOS error-path logging bypasses `os.Logger`, so navigation failures are invisible to `log stream`/Console. *(dimension: logging-rules)* **Fix:** add `Logger(subsystem: "com.scarf", category: "WebviewWidgetView")` and use `logger.warning(...)`.
- **Raw `print()` in iOS WebView delegate (load failure)** — `scarf/Scarf iOS/Projects/Widgets/WebviewWidgetView.swift:121` — same shipped iOS `WKNavigationDelegate` path. *(dimension: logging-rules)* **Fix:** `logger.warning("WebView failed to load: \(error.localizedDescription, privacy: .public)")`.
- **Raw `print()` in macOS WebView delegate (navigation failure)** — `scarf/scarf/Features/Projects/Views/Widgets/WebviewWidgetView.swift:111` — macOS twin of the iOS issue. *(dimension: logging-rules)* **Fix:** add a Coordinator `Logger` and use `logger.warning(...)`.

> The macOS WebView load-failure `print()` at `WebviewWidgetView.swift:115` and the three `HermesFileService` `print()` sites verified at **medium** (below) are the same family of issue — fix all together.

## Recurring patterns (memory-note candidates)

1. **Raw `print()` in production error paths.** Error-handling code (catch blocks, delegate callbacks, validation guards) uses `print("[Scarf] …")` instead of the project-standard `os.Logger`, so failures never reach the structured logging system. Most pervasive finding this cycle; appears identically across macOS and iOS twins. Sites: `HermesFileService.swift:599/659/2006`, `WebviewWidgetView.swift:111/115` (macOS), `Scarf iOS/.../WebviewWidgetView.swift:117/121` (iOS).
   → **Suggested note:** "No raw print() — always os.Logger" (`conventions/`).
2. **`Task.detached` with implicit `self` capture.** Detached tasks open without a capture list, then reference `self`/its properties inside an inner `MainActor.run`, implicitly capturing `self` across the isolation boundary. The codebase already uses `[weak self]` correctly in the same files — drift, not ignorance. Sites: `MCPServersViewModel.swift` (9 sites; correct at L63), `PluginsViewModel.swift:110,140` (correct at L46), `RichChatInputBar.swift:535,574`.
   → **Suggested note:** "Task.detached capture lists must be explicit" (`conventions/`).
3. **`fatalError`/force-unwrap for "programmer error" in code with live error paths.** Non-throwing helpers crash on conditions documented as "programmer error" even though callers already sit inside `try`/catch. Sites: `SQLValueInliner.swift:71,82` (called from `RemoteSQLiteBackend` `try` blocks); `MCPServerPresetPickerView.swift:126` (benign variant).
   → **Suggested note:** "Prefer throwing over fatalError when callers can catch" (`architecture/`).

## Healthy areas
- **Logging convention is real and widely followed.** `HermesFileService` uses `Self.logger.warning(...)` at lines 525/545/1519/1523/1830/1842/1953/1975; the `print()` sites are exceptions against a consistent backdrop. 26+ `ScarfCore` loggers correctly use the `"com.scarf"` subsystem.
- **Concurrency discipline is understood and mostly applied.** The correct `[weak self]` pattern appears right next to the violations; transport mutable state is actor-protected (`CitadelServerTransport.ConnectionHolder`).
- **Security & data-layer invariants hold.** No SSH/Process command-injection, no secret leakage to logs, no `http://` credential paths, no OAuth/Nous CSRF gaps surfaced — and crucially **no writes to `state.db`** and **no disallowed file writes**. `@unchecked Sendable` is generally backed by documented immutability (`ResultBox` lines 565–567).
